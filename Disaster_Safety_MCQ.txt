1. COLDWAVE
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. During a coldwave, what is the MOST important thing to wear when going outside?
A) Light cotton clothes
B) Layered warm clothes including woolen/thermal wear
C) Raincoat
D) Shorts and t-shirt
Answer: B

Q2. Which of the following foods helps your body stay warm during a coldwave?
A) Cold drinks and ice cream
B) Salads and raw vegetables
C) Hot soups, tea, and high-energy foods like nuts
D) Frozen foods
Answer: C

Q3. If you are indoors during a coldwave, what should you do to keep heat inside?
A) Open all windows for fresh air
B) Keep doors and windows sealed/covered to prevent drafts
C) Use a fan to circulate air
D) Sit near open doors
Answer: B

Q4. Hypothermia is a serious risk during a coldwave. What is hypothermia?
A) A skin rash caused by cold air
B) When the body loses heat faster than it can produce, causing dangerously low body temperature
C) A type of cold allergy
D) Frozen water pipes
Answer: B

Q5. Which of the following people are MOST at risk during a coldwave?
A) Young adults and teenagers
B) Elderly people, infants, and people with heart conditions
C) Athletes
D) People who exercise regularly
Answer: B

Q6. What should you do if you see someone shivering uncontrollably and confused during a coldwave?
A) Give them cold water
B) Leave them alone to rest
C) Bring them indoors, cover with blankets, and call for medical help
D) Ask them to exercise outside
Answer: C

Q7. During a coldwave, why should you avoid alcohol to stay warm?
A) Alcohol makes you too energetic
B) Alcohol makes your body feel warm but actually causes it to lose heat faster
C) Alcohol is expensive
D) Alcohol causes thirst
Answer: B

Q8. What should you do to protect pipes in your home during a coldwave?
A) Pour cold water through them regularly
B) Insulate pipes and let a small trickle of water run to prevent freezing
C) Turn off all water completely
D) Leave them as they are
Answer: B

Q9. During a coldwave, which of the following is the safest heating option indoors?
A) Open fire without ventilation
B) Burning coal inside a closed room
C) Electric heater or safe indoor heating with proper ventilation
D) Burning plastic materials
Answer: C

Q10. What emergency number or action should you take if someone collapses from cold exposure?
A) Wait for them to wake up on their own
B) Give them a cold shower
C) Call emergency services immediately and keep them warm while waiting
D) Take them outside for fresh air
Answer: C

2. CYCLONE
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. What is the first thing you should do when a cyclone warning is issued in your area?
A) Go to the beach to watch the waves
B) Stay indoors, listen to official weather updates, and prepare an emergency kit
C) Drive around to see the storm
D) Open all windows for ventilation
Answer: B

Q2. During a cyclone, where is the SAFEST place to take shelter?
A) Near windows to watch the storm
B) Under a tree
C) In a strong, pucca building away from the coast, in an inner room
D) On the rooftop
Answer: C

Q3. What is the "eye" of a cyclone?
A) The most dangerous and windy part
B) A calm, clear center — but dangerous conditions return after it passes
C) A warning signal given by authorities
D) The area where rain begins
Answer: B

Q4. Before a cyclone hits, what should you store in your emergency kit?
A) Only money and jewelry
B) Drinking water, food, medicines, torch, and important documents
C) Electronics and gadgets
D) Textbooks
Answer: B

Q5. Why should you stay away from the coast and rivers during a cyclone?
A) Because it is cold there
B) Storm surges can cause dangerous flooding and waves that sweep people away
C) Because there is no shelter nearby
D) Because fish may attack
Answer: B

Q6. After the cyclone passes, which of the following should you AVOID?
A) Checking on neighbors
B) Listening to official radio updates
C) Walking through floodwater, as it may be contaminated or hide dangers
D) Staying indoors until cleared
Answer: C

Q7. What should you do with loose objects around your home before a cyclone?
A) Leave them outside
B) Bring them indoors or tie them down so they don't become dangerous projectiles
C) Throw them away
D) Give them to neighbors
Answer: B

Q8. During a cyclone, why should you NOT use candles if the power goes out?
A) Candles are too expensive
B) Candles can cause fires, especially with strong winds and damaged structures
C) Candles don't provide enough light
D) Candles melt too quickly
Answer: B

Q9. If you are caught outside during a cyclone, what should you do?
A) Run towards the sea
B) Climb a tree for safety
C) Lie flat in a low-lying area away from trees and power lines
D) Stand under a building overhang
Answer: C

Q10. What is a "storm surge" associated with cyclones?
A) Heavy rainfall during the cyclone
B) A sudden rise in sea level that causes dangerous coastal flooding
C) Strong winds near the eye
D) Thunder and lightning during the storm
Answer: B

3. DROUGHT
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. What is the MOST important step individuals can take to help during a drought?
A) Use more water than usual to store it
B) Conserve water by fixing leaks, taking short showers, and reusing water
C) Dig new wells immediately
D) Ignore the situation
Answer: B

Q2. Which of the following crops is BEST suited to grow during a drought?
A) Rice, which needs lots of water
B) Drought-resistant crops like millets, sorghum, and pulses
C) Sugarcane
D) Water-intensive vegetables
Answer: B

Q3. During a drought, drinking water may become scarce. How should you ensure your water is safe?
A) Drink directly from ponds or rivers
B) Boil water or use purification tablets before drinking
C) Drink any available water without checking
D) Use water from puddles
Answer: B

Q4. What is "water harvesting" and why is it important during a drought?
A) Buying water from shops
B) Collecting and storing rainwater for future use to reduce drought impact
C) Wasting water during monsoon season
D) Drilling deep wells only
Answer: B

Q5. During a severe drought, which government resource should you contact for help?
A) Local police station only
B) District disaster management authority or local panchayat/municipality for relief
C) Private businesses only
D) No one — handle it alone
Answer: B

Q6. How can you save water while brushing your teeth?
A) Keep the tap running the whole time
B) Use a full bucket of water
C) Turn off the tap while brushing and only use water to rinse
D) Use a hose
Answer: C

Q7. Drought can lead to which of the following health risks?
A) Frostbite
B) Dehydration, malnutrition, and waterborne diseases from unsafe water sources
C) Hypothermia
D) Lightning strikes
Answer: B

Q8. What should farmers do to protect their soil during a drought?
A) Leave soil bare and uncovered
B) Use mulching to retain soil moisture and prevent evaporation
C) Burn crop residue
D) Over-irrigate remaining water
Answer: B

Q9. Why is it important NOT to waste food during a drought?
A) Food prices go up so it saves money
B) Food production decreases during drought — wasting food worsens the shortage for everyone
C) Food tastes bad during drought
D) It is only important for farmers
Answer: B

Q10. Which of the following is a long-term solution to prevent drought effects?
A) Cutting down forests
B) Planting trees, building check dams, and promoting water conservation habits
C) Building more roads
D) Using more groundwater without limit
Answer: B

4. EARTHQUAKE
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. During an earthquake, if you are INDOORS, what should you do immediately?
A) Run outside quickly
B) Drop to your hands and knees, take cover under a sturdy table, and hold on
C) Stand near windows
D) Use the elevator to get out
Answer: B

Q2. Why should you NOT run outside during an earthquake?
A) It is too cold outside
B) Falling debris, glass, and collapsing structures near exits can injure you
C) You may get lost
D) Running is tiring
Answer: B

Q3. If you are outdoors during an earthquake, what should you do?
A) Stand near buildings and walls
B) Move away from buildings, trees, streetlights, and power lines, then drop and cover your head
C) Lie on the road
D) Run as fast as possible
Answer: B

Q4. What is an "aftershock" and how should you respond?
A) A warning alarm after the earthquake — go outside
B) Smaller tremors that follow the main quake — stay in your safe position and be cautious
C) The sound of the earthquake — plug your ears
D) A flood that comes after an earthquake
Answer: B

Q5. After an earthquake, why should you check for gas leaks before turning on lights?
A) Gas leaks are harmless
B) A spark from a light switch can ignite leaking gas and cause an explosion or fire
C) Lights don't work after earthquakes
D) Gas leaks make lights flicker
Answer: B

Q6. If you are trapped under debris after an earthquake, what should you do?
A) Shout continuously to use all your energy
B) Stay calm, cover your mouth with cloth, tap on pipes or walls to signal rescuers
C) Move as much as possible to free yourself
D) Try to dig through heavy debris alone
Answer: B

Q7. What emergency supplies should every family keep ready in an earthquake-prone area?
A) Only a first aid kit
B) Water, food, first aid kit, torch, whistle, copies of documents, and medicines
C) Extra furniture
D) A generator only
Answer: B

Q8. Which type of building is generally SAFER during an earthquake?
A) Old brick buildings without reinforcement
B) Earthquake-resistant (seismically designed) structures with proper foundations
C) Wooden huts on hillsides
D) Tall glass buildings
Answer: B

Q9. After an earthquake, which of the following water sources is SAFEST to drink from?
A) Tap water immediately
B) Bottled water or water that has been boiled and purified, as pipes may be damaged
C) Water from cracks in the ground
D) Floodwater
Answer: B

Q10. If you are in a crowded public place during an earthquake, what should you do?
A) Rush towards the exit with everyone
B) Drop and cover your head, stay calm, move away from shelves and glass, and exit calmly when shaking stops
C) Jump out of windows
D) Push others aside to get out first
Answer: B

5. EPIDEMIC
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. What is the MOST effective way to prevent the spread of infectious diseases during an epidemic?
A) Avoid all food and water
B) Wash hands regularly with soap and water, wear masks, and maintain distance from sick people
C) Stay in crowded places to build immunity
D) Share medicines with others without consulting a doctor
Answer: B

Q2. During an epidemic, why is it important to follow official health guidelines?
A) Because guidelines are only for doctors
B) Official guidelines are based on expert medical advice and help slow the spread of disease
C) Guidelines don't matter if you feel healthy
D) Only elderly people need to follow guidelines
Answer: B

Q3. What should you do if you develop symptoms of the disease spreading in your area?
A) Ignore the symptoms and go to school or work
B) Isolate yourself, inform a family member, and contact a doctor or health helpline
C) Take any available medicine without consulting a doctor
D) Visit crowded places to distract yourself
Answer: B

Q4. How do vaccines help during an epidemic?
A) Vaccines cure people who are already seriously ill
B) Vaccines train your immune system to fight a specific disease, preventing infection or reducing severity
C) Vaccines are only for children
D) Vaccines spread the disease faster
Answer: B

Q5. What is "quarantine" and when is it used during an epidemic?
A) A type of medicine given during an epidemic
B) Separating people who may have been exposed to a disease to prevent further spread
C) A holiday given during an epidemic
D) A government lockdown for economic reasons
Answer: B

Q6. During a disease outbreak, why should you avoid touching your face, especially eyes, nose, and mouth?
A) It looks impolite
B) Germs from contaminated surfaces can enter your body through these openings
C) It causes headaches
D) It has no connection to disease spread
Answer: B

Q7. Which of the following is the correct way to sneeze or cough during an epidemic?
A) Sneeze into open air towards others
B) Sneeze or cough into a tissue or into your elbow, then wash your hands
C) Hold your sneeze in completely
D) Sneeze into your hands and wipe them on clothing
Answer: B

Q8. Why is it important to drink clean, safe water during an epidemic?
A) Clean water makes medicines work faster
B) Contaminated water can spread waterborne diseases and weaken your immune system
C) Clean water is only needed for cooking
D) Water has no effect on disease spread
Answer: B

Q9. What should you do with correct and incorrect information (rumours) during an epidemic?
A) Share all information you receive on social media
B) Verify information through official health authorities before sharing, and report misinformation
C) Believe everything you read online
D) Ignore all information
Answer: B

Q10. How can you support others in your community during an epidemic?
A) Spread fear and panic
B) Help elderly or sick neighbours get essentials, share verified information, and follow safety rules yourself
C) Hoard medicines and supplies for only your family
D) Avoid all community responsibility
Answer: B

6. FLOOD
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. During a flood warning, what should you do FIRST?
A) Go to the riverside to see the water level
B) Move to higher ground immediately with essential supplies
C) Stay in your basement
D) Continue your normal activities
Answer: B

Q2. Why should you NEVER walk or drive through floodwater?
A) Floodwater is cold
B) Floodwater can be deeper than it appears, fast-moving, contaminated, and may hide open drains or debris
C) Floodwater makes roads slippery only for vehicles
D) It is only dangerous for children
Answer: B

Q3. What essential items should you carry when evacuating due to a flood?
A) Only your phone and charger
B) Drinking water, food, medicines, important documents, torch, and warm clothes
C) Heavy furniture and appliances
D) Only money
Answer: B

Q4. After floodwater recedes, why should you NOT eat food that was in contact with floodwater?
A) Floodwater makes food taste bad
B) Floodwater is highly contaminated with sewage, chemicals, and bacteria that can cause serious illness
C) Floodwater changes the colour of food
D) It is only unsafe for animals
Answer: B

Q5. What should you do if you are trapped in a building during a flood?
A) Go to the basement
B) Move to the highest floor, signal for help using a bright cloth or torch, and call emergency services
C) Swim to safety immediately
D) Stay on the ground floor
Answer: B

Q6. During heavy flooding, which utility should you turn off in your home first?
A) Internet connection
B) Electricity at the main switch to prevent electrocution
C) Air conditioning
D) Television
Answer: B

Q7. How can communities help prevent flood damage before it happens?
A) Build houses on riverbanks
B) Plant trees, maintain drainage systems, build embankments, and avoid blocking natural water channels
C) Remove all vegetation near rivers
D) Build roads over natural streams
Answer: B

Q8. After a flood, what is the SAFEST source of drinking water?
A) Floodwater that has settled
B) Sealed bottled water or water that has been boiled and properly purified
C) River water that looks clear
D) Rainwater collected in open containers
Answer: B

Q9. What disease can commonly spread after a flood?
A) Malaria, dengue, cholera, and typhoid due to contaminated water and mosquito breeding
B) Frostbite
C) Heatstroke
D) Lung cancer
Answer: A

Q10. If you see a person being swept by floodwater, what is the SAFEST way to help?
A) Jump in after them immediately
B) Throw a rope, floating object, or extend a stick to them — avoid entering the water yourself
C) Wait for them to swim to you
D) Walk into the water to grab them
Answer: B

7. HEATWAVE
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. During a heatwave, when is it SAFEST to go outdoors?
A) Between 12 PM and 3 PM when the sun is strongest
B) Early morning or evening when temperatures are lower
C) At noon for maximum sunlight
D) Anytime — heatwaves don't affect healthy people
Answer: B

Q2. What is "heat stroke" and why is it dangerous?
A) A mild headache from being in the sun
B) A life-threatening condition where body temperature rises above 40°C and the body can no longer cool itself
C) Feeling sleepy in summer
D) A sunburn on the skin
Answer: B

Q3. What should you drink to stay safe during a heatwave?
A) Alcohol and energy drinks
B) Plenty of water, ORS, buttermilk, and fresh juices throughout the day
C) Sugary sodas
D) Only coffee and tea
Answer: B

Q4. Why should you wear light-colored, loose cotton clothing during a heatwave?
A) Because it looks fashionable
B) Light colors reflect sunlight and loose cotton allows sweat to evaporate, keeping your body cooler
C) Dark clothes are too expensive in summer
D) Cotton is waterproof
Answer: B

Q5. Which group of people is MOST vulnerable during a heatwave?
A) Teenagers and young adults
B) Elderly people, infants, outdoor workers, and people with chronic illnesses
C) Athletes in good physical shape
D) People who swim regularly
Answer: B

Q6. If someone collapses due to heat stroke, what should you do IMMEDIATELY?
A) Give them alcohol to drink
B) Move them to a cool place, apply cool water or wet cloth to their skin, and call for medical help
C) Cover them with a thick blanket
D) Ask them to walk around in the sun
Answer: B

Q7. How can you cool your home during a heatwave without air conditioning?
A) Keep curtains open all day to let sunlight in
B) Close curtains/blinds during the day, open windows at night, and use fans to circulate air
C) Cook food inside to keep the kitchen warm
D) Use heaters to balance the temperature
Answer: B

Q8. What is "heat exhaustion" and what are its warning signs?
A) Feeling slightly warm — no action needed
B) A condition caused by overheating, with symptoms like heavy sweating, weakness, dizziness, and nausea
C) A type of skin rash from sunlight
D) Feeling cold despite hot weather
Answer: B

Q9. Why should you NEVER leave children or pets in a parked car during a heatwave?
A) They might damage the car
B) Car interiors heat up extremely quickly and can reach fatal temperatures within minutes
C) Cars are too noisy for children
D) It is only dangerous if windows are open
Answer: B

Q10. How can communities help vulnerable people during a heatwave?
A) Ignore people who live alone
B) Check on elderly neighbors, set up community cooling centers, and distribute water to those in need
C) Avoid all outdoor activity including helping others
D) Only hospitals are responsible for heatwave relief
Answer: B

8. LANDSLIDE
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. Which of the following is a WARNING SIGN that a landslide may occur?
A) Clear skies and dry weather
B) Cracks appearing in the ground or walls, unusual sounds like cracking trees, tilting of trees or poles
C) Sudden drop in temperature
D) Strong winds without rain
Answer: B

Q2. During a landslide, if you are indoors, what should you do?
A) Go outside to see what is happening
B) Move to the upper floors, stay away from windows, and go to an interior room away from the slope
C) Stay on the ground floor near windows
D) Open all doors and windows
Answer: B

Q3. If a landslide is approaching and you are outdoors, what is the BEST action?
A) Run parallel to the slope to escape the path of the slide
B) Climb to higher ground or move to the side of the landslide's path, away from the moving debris
C) Stand and take videos
D) Run down the hill
Answer: B

Q4. After a landslide, why should you stay away from the affected area?
A) The mud smells bad
B) Additional slides, unstable ground, downed power lines, and contaminated water pose ongoing dangers
C) Rescue teams prefer to work alone
D) The area is permanently safe after the first slide
Answer: B

Q5. What human activity increases the risk of landslides?
A) Planting trees on slopes
B) Deforestation, construction on steep slopes, and improper waste disposal on hillsides
C) Building check dams
D) Contour farming
Answer: B

Q6. How does heavy rainfall trigger landslides?
A) Rain makes the soil too hard
B) Rainwater saturates the soil, adding weight and reducing friction, causing the slope to give way
C) Rain erodes rocks but doesn't affect soil
D) Rain only causes flooding, not landslides
Answer: B

Q7. If your area is under a landslide watch or warning, what should you do?
A) Continue all normal activities
B) Be ready to evacuate, prepare an emergency kit, and follow instructions from local authorities
C) Move to a riverside area
D) Dig trenches around your home
Answer: B

Q8. What is the SAFEST place to shelter if a landslide occurs while you are driving on a mountain road?
A) Stop the car on the road and wait inside
B) Pull off the road quickly, get out, and move to higher stable ground away from the vehicle
C) Drive as fast as possible through the landslide zone
D) Park under a bridge
Answer: B

Q9. How do trees and plant roots help prevent landslides?
A) They block sunlight and increase soil heat
B) Tree roots hold the soil together, reduce water runoff, and stabilize slopes
C) Trees add weight to slopes and make them more dangerous
D) Plants have no effect on slope stability
Answer: C

Q10. After a landslide, what is the safest way to check if your building is safe to re-enter?
A) Enter immediately to check your belongings
B) Wait for official inspection by government engineers or disaster management teams before re-entering
C) Ask a neighbor if it looks fine from outside
D) Check only the ground floor before entering
Answer: B

9. LIGHTNING
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. During a lightning storm, which place is the SAFEST to take shelter?
A) Under a tall tree
B) Inside a solid building or a hard-topped metal vehicle with windows closed
C) In an open field
D) Near a swimming pool or lake
Answer: B

Q2. If you are outdoors and cannot find shelter during a lightning storm, what should you do?
A) Stand on high ground with arms raised
B) Crouch low with feet together, hands over ears, and avoid lying flat on the ground
C) Lie flat on the ground
D) Stand under the tallest tree nearby
Answer: B

Q3. Why should you avoid using a mobile phone outdoors during a lightning storm?
A) Mobile phones attract lightning directly
B) Using a mobile phone outdoors keeps you stationary and exposed; wired phones are more dangerous than mobiles, but staying outside is the key risk
C) Mobile phones stop working in storms
D) Mobile phones make lightning louder
Answer: B

Q4. What is the "30-30 rule" for lightning safety?
A) Wait 30 seconds after lightning, then run for 30 meters
B) If thunder follows lightning in 30 seconds or less, seek shelter; wait 30 minutes after the last thunder before going outside
C) Count 30 lightning strikes before taking shelter
D) Lightning is safe after 30 minutes of rain
Answer: B

Q5. Why should you stay away from water bodies during a lightning storm?
A) Water makes lightning invisible
B) Water is an excellent conductor of electricity — lightning striking near water can electrocute anyone in or near it
C) Water bodies attract tornadoes
D) Water reduces the sound of thunder
Answer: B

Q6. If someone is struck by lightning, which of the following is TRUE?
A) They will remain electrified — do not touch them
B) They are safe to touch; call for help immediately and perform CPR if trained and if needed
C) Give them water immediately
D) Leave them alone and call emergency services only
Answer: B

Q7. Which of the following should you AVOID during a lightning storm indoors?
A) Sitting on a sofa away from windows
B) Using corded telephones, touching electrical appliances, plumbing fixtures, or standing near windows
C) Closing windows and doors
D) Staying on the ground floor
Answer: B

Q8. Why is standing under a tree during lightning dangerous?
A) Trees block the rain and make you cold
B) Lightning frequently strikes tall trees; the electrical current travels down the trunk and can strike anyone nearby
C) Trees sway and may scratch you
D) Trees only attract lightning in forests
Answer: B

Q9. If you are swimming or in a boat when a lightning storm begins, what should you do?
A) Continue swimming — water is safe during storms
B) Get out of the water and seek shelter immediately — water is extremely dangerous during lightning
C) Dive underwater to stay safe
D) Move to the center of the lake
Answer: B

Q10. What is "ground current" in the context of lightning?
A) The underground power cables activated during lightning
B) Electricity that spreads outward along the ground from a lightning strike point, which can electrocute people or animals nearby
C) A type of lightning that travels horizontally
D) Static electricity generated by storm clouds
Answer: B

10. WILDFIRE
----------------------------------------------------------------

Q1. When a wildfire warning is issued near your area, what should you do FIRST?
A) Wait to see if the fire reaches your street
B) Prepare to evacuate, gather emergency supplies, and follow instructions from local authorities
C) Stay indoors with windows open
D) Go towards the fire to get a better view
Answer: B

Q2. Which direction should you move to escape a wildfire?
A) Into the wind (upwind), as smoke travels downwind
B) Across or away from the path of the fire, perpendicular to its direction of travel
C) Toward dense forest for shade
D) Uphill, as fire moves slowly uphill
Answer: B

Q3. Why do wildfires spread FASTER uphill?
A) Wind blows more strongly uphill
B) Heat rises, preheating vegetation above the fire, and steep slopes bring unburned fuel closer to flames
C) Water runs downhill away from the fire
D) Trees are taller on hills
Answer: B

Q4. If you are caught in a wildfire and cannot escape, what should you do?
A) Climb a tree to stay above the flames
B) Find a cleared area or ditch, lie face down, cover yourself with soil, and breathe through a wet cloth close to the ground
C) Run as fast as possible through the flames
D) Stand in a river only
Answer: B

Q5. What should you do to prepare your home if you live in a wildfire-prone area?
A) Plant dry shrubs and trees close to the house for shade
B) Clear dry leaves, brush, and flammable materials around your home to create a "defensible space"
C) Store extra fuel near the house
D) Leave wooden furniture on the porch
Answer: B

Q6. How does smoke from a wildfire affect your health?
A) Smoke only affects people with severe allergies
B) Wildfire smoke contains harmful particles and gases that can cause breathing problems, eye irritation, and worsen heart and lung conditions
C) Smoke is only dangerous when standing directly in it
D) Smoke has no health effects in small amounts
Answer: B

Q7. During a wildfire evacuation, which route should you follow?
A) Any road that seems clear of traffic
B) The official evacuation route announced by authorities — other roads may be cut off by fire
C) The shortest route on your map app
D) Go through the forest to avoid traffic
Answer: B

Q8. If smoke enters your home during a wildfire, what should you do?
A) Open windows to let it out
B) Close all windows, doors, and vents; seal gaps with wet towels; use an air purifier if available
C) Turn on ceiling fans to circulate air
D) Spray water inside the house
Answer: B

Q9. What role do humans most often play in starting wildfires?
A) Humans rarely cause wildfires — they are almost always natural
B) Unattended campfires, burning of agricultural waste, discarded cigarettes, and arson are among the leading human causes
C) Wildfires only start from lightning
D) Humans only spread wildfires, they don't start them
Answer: B

Q10. After returning home following a wildfire, what should you be cautious about?
A) Turning on all lights at once to check the house
B) Checking for hot spots, damaged structures, downed power lines, and contaminated water before settling in
C) Assuming the house is safe since the fire has passed
D) Immediately airing out the house by opening all windows
Answer: B
